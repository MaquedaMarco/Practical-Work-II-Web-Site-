Fundamentals of Computer Engineering

Subject Description:


"Fundamentals of Computer Engineering" aims to provide a global vision of Computer Engineering in both academic and professional points of view. The course gives the students a grasp of the fundamentals on which this engineering is based (mathematics, physics, anthropology and ethics), the future professional careers and the role of a computer engineer in today's society.

Goals:


The specific objectives that this subject aims to achieve are:

  1. To understand the purpose of Computer Engineering, its ultimate goal, and the societal benefits it provides, while reflecting on the role each individual wishes to play as an engineer in the future society.

  2. To gain knowledge about the pillars on which technological progress must be based, including a historical perspective on technological development.

  3. To recognize the importance of the human factor in professional practice and foster a sense of service and contribution to the common good.

  4. To understand the purpose of Computer Engineering, its ultimate goal, and the societal benefits it provides, while reflecting on the role each individual wishes to play as an engineer in the future society.

COURSE SYLLABUS:



MODULE I: THEORETICAL FOUNDATIONS

BLOCK 1. Computer Engineering in Society.

  1. Historical background and perspectives.

  2. Definitions and basic concepts.

  3. Past, present, and future of ICT (Information and Communication Technology).

  4. Computer Engineering as an academic discipline and profession: (1) competencies and knowledge required for a computer engineer; (2) career prospects; (3) the role of ethics in the education of computer engineers; and (4) code of ethics.

BLOCK 2. Current paradigms and trends in systems and applications.

  1. Software development: more than just programming.

  2. Structure of computer systems: hardware, software, Operative systems and networks.

  3. New paradigms and trends: Cloud computing, Quantum computing, Artificial Intelligence, Blockchain, Smart cities, IoT (Internet of Things).

BLOCK 3. Information and Data.

  1. Information and Data: number systems, representation of numeric and alphanumeric information (codes).

  2. Data Foundations: Big Data, Data models, Data protection, Laws and ethics.


MODULE II: PRACTICAL SKILLS - INTRODUCTION TO WEB DEVELOPMENT

BLOCK 1. Introduction to and management of software repositories.

  1. Basic concepts.

  2. Repositories: Git & Github.

BLOCK 2. Creation of HTML5 web pages.

  1. Basic concepts.

  2. The Document Object Model.

  3. Forms and containers.

BLOCK 3. Design and layout with CSS3.

  1. Basic concepts.

  2. CSS elements.

  3. Box and float models.

  4. Element positioning.
