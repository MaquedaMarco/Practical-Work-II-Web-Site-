Calculating the long term probability of the boardgame Monopoly using Markov Chains and Code

When I was finishing my baccalaureate degree, on my mathematics class I had to send a "Mathematical Exploration Essay" which is in essence an essay about a mathematical topic of my choosing. This was worth 25% of the final grade

I decided to make mine about calculating the long term probability of the boardg game Monopoly.

I did this beacuse of two reasons:

1.- It allowed me to study Markov Chains, a process that uses Matrcies to calculate the long term probabilities of events. As I didn't have matrices on my syllabus, I thought it made a good match.

2.- I would put my coding test skills to the test as I hadn't made any programming since 4 years prior, and what I knew was from a summer when I watched around 5 youtube videos on how to programm on C#.

Coming up next are part of the process and the conclusion of the essay, plus some further reading for those interested.

This is the graph that represetnts the Long-Term Probability of ending up on a given square of the board against the assaigned number for that square.

This probabilties where obtained using Markov chains, which by the way created a 40x40 matrix, that had to me evaluated to the limit when n tends to infinity when that matrix is to the power of n, which was something that went way beyond my mathematical understanding at the time.

If you are curious, the most landed square was jail, and the least the chance squres.
Allthouigh is important to note that the probabilities are of ENDING on a given square, not first landing there, then you move if you have to.

This is the same graph from earlier, plus the long-term probability calculated using the code I created.

At the time I didn't know about Arrays, or how While loops really worked, so this translated on apart from a very inefficient code, that instead of giving me the probabilities of the simulation it gave me the raw numbers, and I had to copy 10 000 of them to later using another program count how many times a square appeared on the code. This made my programm crash more times that I would like to admit.

And as you can rpobablly imagine, it made the whole process of data collecting quite cumbersome.
